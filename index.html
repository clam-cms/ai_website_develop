Artificial Intelligence at CMS

Artificial Intelligence at the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services

CMS has long recognized the potential of Artificial Intelligence (AI) to revolutionize healthcare delivery and operations. Our efforts have yielded insights captured in the CMS AI Playbook, a comprehensive guide that continues to improve along with our understanding of AI best practices. This site offers access to the CMS AI Playbook and other federal resources, equipping the CMS community and partners with knowledge to leverage AI responsibly and effectively.

CMS AI Playbook

The CMS AI Playbook provides readers a one-stop shop to dive responsibly into AI development, ensuring CMS teams are equipped to use AI responsibly and effectively.

Human-Centered AI

Human-centered AI (HCAI) addresses the needs and prioritizes the well-being of end users and society, emphasizing the human-centered practices throughout the design and development process.

Well-Grounded & Data Driven AI

Well-grounded and data-driven AI addresses the needs of AI tools and models, ensuring that they are based on the right information for the right task.

Appropriately Scaled & Interoperable AI

Appropriately scaled and interoperable AI addresses the needs of agency integration, ensuring AI adoption within CMS is incremental and can adapt to the agencyâ€™s needs.

Responsible AI

Responsible AI (RAI) addresses the needs of industry ethics, applying trustworthy best practices and federal guidance to balance the complexity of moral principles and system requirements.

Aligning AI with CMS Goals

As AI maturity increases across the organization, we aim to align AI with CMS goals to advance healthcare quality, accessibility, efficiency, and affordability for Medicare and Medicaid beneficiaries.

Enhance Operational Efficiency

Enhancing operational efficiency, such as automating administrative tasks, streamlining claims processing, and optimizing resource allocation

Make Data Driven Decisions

Managing health care data more effectively, enabling CMS to make data-driven decisions that improve program management and policy formulation.

Support Equitable Access

Supporting equitable access to healthcare services, addressing disparities, and ensuring beneficiaries receive high-quality care.

Assure Compliance & Quality

Assuring compliance and quality by monitoring healthcare providers' compliance with CMS standards and regulations

Identify Savings & Reduce Waste

Identifying cost-saving opportunities, reducing waste, and making healthcare more affordable for beneficiaries and the government.
